Wildfires within the wildland-urban interface (WUI) represent a growing global threat. Accurate simulation of these events remains a significant challenge due to the complex, multiscale feedback loops between atmospheric turbulence. We introduce Weather-FDS, a novel modelling system that integrates the Parallelized Large-eddy Simulation Model (PALM) with the Fire Dynamics Simulator (FDS) to capture these dynamics across scales ranging from large circulation patterns to localised turbulence. The methodology utilises a one-way offline coupling interface where FDS is forced by evolving weather fields generated by PALM. We developed specialised processing scripts to read PALM outputs for temperature and velocity, interpolate them as needed, and generate the necessary initial and boundary condition files for FDS and have adapted FDS code to be forced by these fields. 

Validation was conducted through two primary experiments. Experiment 1 evaluated coupled performance in idealised domains without fire, testing various weather forcing intervals (1 s, 10 s, and 60 s) and boundary configurations. We found that coupling all lateral and top boundaries is essential to capture accurate inflow variability. Furthermore, high-frequency PALM output was not strictly required, as linear interpolation in time between forcing fields proved sufficient for this setup. Experiment 2 applied Weather-FDS to idealised fire scenarios using a Level Set Method for tall grass and timber fuels. The coupled simulations successfully captured shifts in wind direction emanating from weather dependent variability and their subsequent impact on fire front propagation. Weather-FDS did not produce symmetrical counter-rotating vortices observed in standalone non-coupled runs, providing a more realistic representation of fire-atmosphere interactions in the convective boundary layer.  

This work establishes a robust multi-scale workflow to advance research into extreme wildfire behaviour and fire modelling.
