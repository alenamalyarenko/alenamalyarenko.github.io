The P-SKRIPS (Polar-SKRIPS) model is a state-of-the-art, regional coupled framework specifically engineered for the Southern Ocean and Antarctic environments. It integrates the Polar Weather Research and Forecasting (PWRF) model with the MITgcm ocean and sea-ice model using the ESMF/NUOPC coupler.

A hallmark of P-SKRIPS is its rigorous conservation of heat and mass fluxes at the air-sea-ice interface, a feature that significantly reduces model drift compared to non-coupled systems. This high-resolution coupling is essential for investigating the dynamics of coastal polynyas and evaluating basal melt rates under ice shelves, such as the Ross Ice Shelf. By capturing small-scale turbulent exchanges and feedback loops, P-SKRIPS provides a robust tool for simulating the complex interactions that drive Antarctic climate variability.
